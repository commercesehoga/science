Preparation Strategy

01
NCERT Line-by-Line First

Every verified question from this window traces directly to NCERT text, examples, or back exercises. Tables, in-text examples, and footnotes are equally important. Do not skip NCERT in favour of guide books for CUET.

02
Electrochemistry and Kinetics Formulas

Nernst equation, Kohlrausch law, rate law expressions, half-life formulas, and Arrhenius equation appeared across multiple days. Practice quick substitution, not derivation under exam pressure.

03
Organic Reaction Sequences

Named reactions, reagent identification (alcohol to aldehyde, nitrobenzene conversions), and IUPAC naming were consistent scorers. Build a quick-recall reagent chart and revise it separately.

04
Coordination and d-block Facts

Oxidation states, especially Cr in K2Cr2O7 and Mn compounds, coordination number rules, hybridization patterns, and isomerism types were repeated. Memorize these as static facts for fast recall.

05
Speed Over Completeness

With -1 per wrong answer, accuracy matters more than attempting every question. Set a personal skip threshold, do not dwell on uncertain items, and always revisit with remaining time rather than guessing immediately.

06
Acidity, Basicity, and Hybridization

Acidic strength orders, strongest base identification, hybridization in ethyne and other molecules, and orbital quantum numbers were easy scoring questions when known. These are direct recall items with no calculation required.
